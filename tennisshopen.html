Redesigning an
e-commerce site
From slow download time, information overload and product oriented dinosaurs, e-commerce sites have developed greatly in the last decade.

To user friendly, accessible and swift layers of integrated systems that gives customers a smooth shopping experience.
So how does one set out to redesign a e-commerce site?

In a quick school project, I and a team of additional 3 students set out to redesign the local Gothenburg company site and dito e-commerce site tennishopen.se ("The Tennis Shop").

The aim was to improve the site with the user/customer in mind. No user data was supplied.
My role - besides taking part in all parts of the redesign - became that of considering the business impact of the redesign, and suggesting to alter the brand.

RESEARCH
Aquaintance
Tennisshopen was founded in 1976 in Gothenburg and has over the years grown from a physical store only to offering its customers an online store with clothes and accessories for tennis and padel.

The founders and individuals associated with the company are experts in tennis, many with a professional player background.
Analysing the web content, Instagram and Facebook flows as well as reviews on Google indicates that customers consider the expertise and high service level to be one reason they shop at Tennishopen.

The above image is, however, not apparent on the site today. The visuals, tonality and pricing could easily be confused with a low-end, price-only focused competing e-commerce site. There is a shortage in user focus and an inconsequent navigation structure.
User experience issues
Before digging into ideation and design, we wanted to test
some user scenarios to examine how one of us would rate the
user experience of the site. We stated four user stories and
used the desired action as a scenario to execute on the site as
it looks today.
User stories
1. As a tennis novice without previous experience of tennis equipment
I want to find out which racket suits me
So that I know that I have the right equipment
2. As a tennis enthusiast
I want to buy tennis clothes at a low cost
Because I play a lot of tennis and the clothes get worn down
3. As a customer at Tennisshopen
I want to find information on complaints
So that I can return a product
4. As a representative for a tennis association
I want to find information on group discount
So that we can buy new equipment to our children groups
Key Findings from Scenario User Testing
1. Ease of adaptation for new users is low
2. Unnecessarily complex navigation structure
3. The copy includes technical terms that puzzles novice users
4. Price matching feature risks customers loose loyalty
Competitive analysis
Following the scenario tests, we wanted to get a picture of what the companys customers can shop elsewhere.
So we visited a number of randomly picked competitors.
IDEATION
The conclusion of the primary research and short user experiance tests indicate that there are elements that Tennishopen would gain from redesigning. Even more so, it indicates that tennishopen.se might gain from a rebranding of the company by positioning it as a high-end, expert and service oriented business.
Below are the elements we found and would propose to the company. And a moodboard, illustrating the visual image we are looking for, as well as design assets.
High-end visual/brand identity
The change in visual impression is more true to the core of Tennisshopen´s value proposition and target groups.

This will probably increase the appeal to high-spending customers (that is a large proportion of the target groups in tennis). It also alignes with their professionality of service.
Simplified navigation
The navigation is unecessary complex, risking high bounce rate and low conversion rate. Simplifyng navigation will shorten amount of clicks, minimize bounce rate and improve the overall user experience of the site.

For Tenisshopen, this adds customer value. Easier adaptation for new visitors lessens and existing customers experience improves, increasing retainment.
Instructive guide(s)
Using guides is a proficient way of simplifying the site adaptation for new customers and tennis players. As well as a great service.

Using their expertise, guides can be used as tips and tricks in the sport as well as guiding their visitors to what equipment suits them.
Leverage competitive advantage
Tennishopen is a high-end e-commerce site with profound expertise and a high degree of service level. They should leverage their competitive advantages.

One way is to storify the about us-text and place it more centrally than just a short text in the footer. This will emphasize that Tennishopen are experts in both tennis and customer satisfaction.




Adjust the copy & lenght of parapgraphs
The paragraph vertical length and the amount of textual information renders an information overflow for the user. Paragraphs should be shortened vertically, ideally by using subheaders as links that - when clicking/hovering - reveals additional info.

The copy can be concentrated. For instance, the use of “tennis-” prior to articles or subsites can be deleted. The visitors presumes that shoes sold here are tennis shoes, for example.

LESSONS LEARNED
Complexity of e-commerce
E-commerce is an extremely complex mix of several systems that have to communicate and align. Designing for this context is a challenge!
Branding and design
We early discovered that we needed to suggest not just a redesign but also a rebranding of the company. These areas can clash, so as a designer I have to be careful what to suggest and in what context.
Technical terms
This case illustrates how important it is to think about what terminology the customer uses. You have to talk to the customer, regardless of what technical terms you want to use internally.
